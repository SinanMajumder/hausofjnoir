The 5 Staffing Compliance Mistakes That Trigger California Citations — And How to Fix Them Before Your Next LPA Visit

California's Title 22 training requirements are more specific than most administrators realize. Here's exactly what Licensing Program Analysts check — and where most facilities fall short.

The staffing crisis is real — but the compliance problem hiding behind it is often worse. Facilities scrambling to fill shifts sometimes cut corners on training documentation, and that's precisely where LPAs focus their inspections in 2026. Each of the five issues below has resulted in citations at California RCFE, ARF, and Adult Day facilities in the past 12 months.

1

The 40-hour training clock starts on Day 1 — not when paperwork is complete.
All direct care staff must complete 40 hours of initial training before working independently with residents. If your new hire is assisting with ADLs before those hours are documented, you are out of compliance — even if the employee has years of prior experience at another facility.
2

Medication aide shadowing has a hard deadline most facilities miss.
In facilities with 16 or more residents, medication aides must complete 24 hours of training — 16 of which must be hands-on shadowing — within the first four weeks. Facilities with 15 or fewer residents: 10 hours total, 6 hands-on, within two weeks. Verbal sign-off is not sufficient — everything must be documented in writing.
3

Annual continuing education is tracked per employee — not per facility.
Twenty hours of annual continuing education are required for every direct care staff member individually. CCLD reviews individual logs, not facility totals. One employee short of 20 hours is a citation.
4

Person-centered care language is now required in training content — not just delivery.
A 2025 regulatory update requires all staff training to emphasize behavioral expressions and person-centered care approaches for all residents, regardless of diagnosis. LPAs are reviewing training materials, not just logs.
5

Agency staff are your compliance responsibility — not the agency's.
Every employee providing care at your facility — including temporary agency staff — must have DSS and FBI criminal background clearance before resident contact. You are responsible for verifying and documenting clearance for every person in your facility, regardless of how they are employed.
 
⚠
2025 Regulatory Update - Still Catching Facilities Off Guard

California's 2025 update requires all staff training to address behavioral expressions and person-centered approaches for all residents — not just those with dementia diagnoses. If your training materials predate 2025 or use diagnosis-based framing, update them before your next LPA visit. This is one of the most commonly missed updates in current inspections.

Your Action Checklist

Complete Before Your Next LPA Visit

 
Pull individual training logs for every direct care employee hired in the past 6 months — verify 40 hours documented with dates and supervisor signatures.
Confirm medication aide shadowing was completed within the required window and documented before any resident medication assistance.
Review annual continuing education records per employee — identify anyone short of 20 hours for this calendar year.
Audit training materials for person-centered and behavioral care language — update anything using diagnosis-based or clinical-only framing.
Verify DSS and FBI clearance documentation is on file for every current staff member, including agency staff.
Confirm administrator's CEU pace — 40 hours per 2-year period, including 8 hours of dementia/Alzheimer's content.
Quick Hits — California Compliance Radar
Deadline
Administrator CEUs: If your administrator's two-year certification cycle ends in 2026, 40 classroom hours must be completed — including 8 hours covering Alzheimer's and other dementias. DSS does not send renewal reminders. Confirm the cycle end date now.
New Rule
Firearms disclosure in admission agreements: As of 2025, California RCFEs and ARFs must include a firearms policy and possession disclosure in every admission agreement per Health & Safety Code §1569.282. If your template predates 2025, update it before your next admission.
Inspection
Incident reporting documentation: Falls and hospitalizations now require LIC 624 reporting with both immediate notification and a seven-day written follow-up to physicians and responsible parties. Verbal notification alone is no longer sufficient — all notifications must be documented.
Background
Clearance reminder: DSS and FBI background clearance is required for every person providing care — including short-term agency fills. Your facility bears the compliance obligation. Verify and document clearance before first resident contact, every time.
